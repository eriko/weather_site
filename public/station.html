Weather Station Instruments

Air Temperature and Relative Humidity Probe:

The HMP50-ET is a temperature and relative humidity sensor manufactured by Vaisala. It measures air temperature with a 1000 ohm platinum resistance thermometer (PRT), and relative humidity with a capacitive chip. A radiation shield is used to cover the probe to protect it from sunlight.

Specifications:

Temperature

  • Measurement Range: -25° to +60°C
  • Accuracy of +/- 0.8 º C

    Relative Humidity

  • Operating Range: 0 to 98% RH
  • Accuracy: ±3% (0-90% RH); ±5% (90-98% RH)

    Solar Radiation Sensor:

    The CS305-ETM is a pyranometer manufactured by Apogee Instruments. It measures shortwave radiation using a silicon photovoltaic detector. The range of the sensor is 300 to 1100 nanometers, which encompasses most of the shortwave radiation that reaches the Earth’s surface.

    Specifications:

  • Accuracy: ±5% for daily total radiation
  • Range: 0 to 2000 W m-2
  • Operating Temperature: -40° to +55°C
  • Relative Humidity: 0 to 100%
  • Output of 0.2 mV per Wm-2

    Rain Gage:

    The TE525-ET tipping bucket rain gage is manufactured by Texas Electronics. It measures precipitation in 0.01 inch increments. The precipitation is funneled into a bucket that tips when filled to the calibrated level of 0.01 inches; the magnet connected to the tipping mechanism activates a switch as the bucket tips. This momentary switch closure is counted by the pulse-counting circuitry of the weather station’s data logger.

    Specifications:

  • Temperature: 0° to +50°C
  • Resolution: 1 tip
  • Rainfall per tip: 0.01” (0.254 mm)
  • Orifice diameter: 6.06” (15.4 cm)
  • Accuracy is ±1% at rates up to 1”/hr or +/- accuracy @ 2” per hr (50.8mm per hr) or less

    Anemometer and Vane:

    The 034B-ETM Windset is an instrument that combines a 3-cup anemometer and a wind vane. It is made of aluminum and its purpose is to measure wind speed and direction. The wind speed is measured as the cups rotate- opening and closing a reed switch at a rate corresponding to wind speed. The vane position is conveyed by a 10K ohm potentiometer with the output voltage related to wind direction.

    Specifications

    Anemometer

  • Range: 0 to 110 mph (0 to 50 m/s)
  • Threshold: 0.9 mph (0.4 m/s)
  • Accuracy: ±0.25 mph (0.11 m/s) when less than 22.7 mph (10.1 m/s) or ±1.1% of true when greater than 22.7 mph (10.1 m/s)

    Vane

  • Mechanical range: 360°
  • Electrical range: 356°
  • Threshold: 0.9 mph (0.4 m/s)
  • Accuracy: ±4°
  • Damping ratio: 0.25
  • Resolution: 0.5°

    Solar Panel:

    A 10-Watt solar panel powers the weather station, charging a 7-Ahr rechargeable battery.

    Specifications

  • Current at peak: 0.59 A
  • Voltage at peak: 16.8 V

    Datalogger:

    An environmental enclosure (the white case) protects a CR1000M module and the battery. The datalogger is designed to both control the functions of the weather station instruments and record measurements. A spectrum spread radio then transmits the information to a nearby modem and is connected to the campus system.

    Specifications:

  • Analog inputs: 16 single-ended or 8 differential, individually configured
  • Pulse counters: 2
  • Switched voltage excitations: 3
  • Control/digital ports: 8
  • RS-232 port: 1
  • CS I/O port: 1
  • Scan rate: 100 Hz
  • Burst mode: 1500 Hz
  • Analog volt. resolution: to 0.33 uV
  • A/D bits: 13
  • Programming: CRBasic
  • Data Storage: Table
  • Telecommunications: PakBus
